I delivered with [Diego Miguel Lozano](https://www.diegomiguel.me/) an outreach talk for high-school students at **IES Mutxamel** during Science Week, focused on introducing artificial intelligence from both a technical and social perspective.

The session combined an accessible introduction to AI with a discussion of its broader implications:

* 🤖 **What AI is**: basic intuitions behind machine learning, data, models, and prediction.
* 🌍 **Opportunities of AI**: applications in science, health, education, accessibility, sustainability, and creativity.
* ⚠️ **Risks of AI systems**: bias, discrimination, misinformation, privacy, automation errors, and overreliance on algorithmic outputs.
* 🧠 **Critical AI literacy**: how to use AI tools responsibly, question their outputs, and understand their limitations.
* 🔬 **AI as a scientific career path**: introducing students to research, university studies, and the role of science in building better technologies.

The goal of the talk was to help students move beyond using AI as a black-box tool and start thinking about how these systems are built, evaluated, and deployed in society.
