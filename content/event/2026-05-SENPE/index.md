I participated in a **round table on AI for Science and AI4Health** at the **SENPE Workshop** in Pamplona, focused on the opportunities and risks of applying artificial intelligence to scientific and health-related domains.

The discussion addressed:

* 🧬 **AI for scientific discovery**: how machine learning and generative AI can support hypothesis generation, literature exploration, data analysis, and biomedical research.
* 🏥 **AI4Health applications**: clinical decision support, patient stratification, risk prediction, and personalized interventions.
* 🧪 **Scientific reliability**: reproducibility, validation, uncertainty, evaluation protocols, and the limits of benchmark-driven progress.
* ⚖️ **Responsible and trustworthy AI**: bias, privacy, data governance, transparency, and human oversight in health-related AI systems.
* 🤝 **Interdisciplinary deployment**: the need for collaboration between clinicians, researchers, data scientists, institutions, and regulators.

The round table emphasized that AI can become a powerful tool for science and healthcare only when its deployment is grounded in rigorous validation, domain expertise, and responsible governance.
