## Algorithmic Fairness and Trustworthy AI

I delivered the masterclass **“Algorithmic Fairness and Trustworthy AI”** as part of the second edition of **Encuentros por la Igualdad de Género**, held at the **Museo Arqueológico de Alicante (MARQ)** and focused on the use of artificial intelligence in local public administration from a gender-equality perspective.

The session explored how AI systems can reproduce or amplify discrimination, especially when they are deployed in institutional and high-impact decision-making contexts. It combined technical foundations of algorithmic fairness with a broader discussion of trustworthy AI, public-sector accountability, and the social implications of automated decision-making.

The talk covered:

* 🧮 **What is AI**: dydactic and non-hype explanation of what is AI.
* ⚖️ **Algorithmic discrimination in GenAI, decision-making and social networks**: how AI systems can reproduce, conceal, or amplify social and gender inequalities.
* 🏛️ **Public-sector AI governance**: transparency, accountability, contestability, and responsible institutional adoption.

The session emphasized that trustworthy AI in government requires more than technical performance: it requires institutional responsibility, social awareness, and explicit safeguards against discrimination.
